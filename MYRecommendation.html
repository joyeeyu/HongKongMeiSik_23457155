About Me

As a Hong Konger, I was born and raised in the vibrant city of Hong Kong,
where I have had the pleasure of indulging in a wide variety of local cuisine since my childhood.
Through participating in exchange activities abroad, I have also had the opportunity to savor authentic dishes from different countries.
In the following paragraphs, I will introduce some of the restaurants and dishes that I long for the most when I am away.

I hope you will find them equally enjoyable!


- TamJai Yunnan Mixian -


Let me tell you about the renowned TamJai Yunnan Mixian, a beloved culinary delight that has gained popularity among locals and tourists alike.
This dish has captivated the hearts and taste buds of many due to its unique flavors and comforting appeal. TamJai Yunnan Mixian is known for its signature rice noodles, which are tender and chewy, served in a rich and fragrant broth. Topped with succulent meats, fresh vegetables, and aromatic spices, it offers a harmonious blend of ingredients.
Its popularity has even reached Japan, where TamJai Yunnan Mixian has established its presence, further expanding the reach of this delightful dish.




Tamjai has a variety of mixian toppings to choose from.

If you don’t want to choose from left to right, you can directly choose Cross-bridge Mixian. Cross-bridge Mixian come with 17 kinds of ingredients including pork, ham, fish eggs, beef, etc. After that, you can also choose your favorite soup base from 7 kinds.
If you like spicy food, I recommend you try Hot & Numbing Soup. If you like spicy food and want to be more appetizing, you can choose Hot & Sour. Soup (Spiciness & Sourness are fixed), even if you don’t like spicy food, there are still Tomato Soup, Charred Pepper & Spices Soup with unique flavors, etc.

Finally, there is also a choice of spiciness, allowing you to taste the flavor that suits you best.









Tamjai doesn't stop and keeps innovating flavors.

For example, the golden fish soup mixian with grilled fish fillet and fish belly seen in the clip.







- Cheung Chau -

Let me tell you about the renowned Cheung Chau is the best resort for many Hong Kong people to go on holiday to the outlying islands.
In addition to the rustic fishing villages and superb coastal scenery, there are also many delicacies entertained by Cheung Chau people.
Here are recommended meals, snacks and late-night snacks for you.

Meals


Blue Bird

Restaurant

Cheung Chau Blue Bird's minced oyster and pork porridge is Blue Bird's famous specialty. The portion of oysters is large, and the minced meat, coriander and green onions make the porridge base particularly sweet and fresh. In addition to oyster minced meat porridge, the restaurant also offers cart noodles, which is a good recommendation for Cheung Chau dinner.

No.11

American-style restaurant

It specializes in hamburgers, but also serves all-day breakfast, baked lobster and a variety of drinks. The more famous ones are crab meat baked potatoes with guacamole, mushroom cheeseburger and giant brown mushroom burger, which are very cost-effective. The store is decorated in a stylish style, giving it a leisurely feel. There are also beach chairs and umbrellas outside, giving it a resort feel.

Hing Lok Restaurant

Seafood-restaurant

How can you not taste seafood when you come to the small island of Cheung Chau? You can buy seafood at Cheung Chau market or seafood stalls and then have it processed at Hing Lok Restaurant.





Snacks


Cheung Chau fish ball

Snack-restaurant

The famous specialty of Cheung Chau is of course the large fish eggs. Places selling large fish eggs are concentrated near the pier. The taste is inseparable from spicy, plum flavor, curry, satay, barbecue sauce, etc.

Cheung Chau Corner

Drink

In fact, this MTR wall is the Cheung Chau Corner check-in wall. In fact, the restaurant uses Hong Kong mother's milk as raw material, ferments it in-house, and provides healthy and light cheese, so it is very fresh and quality guaranteed. The cheese comes in a variety of fruit flavors, such as strawberry, mango, etc. It is made into puree and then beaten together with the cheese, so it tastes fresh and sweet.

Mango Mochi

Dessert

Throughout Cheung Chau Island, many mango mochi have been opened, each with its own supporters. The mango mochi of this restaurant are very filling. The mango flesh is full, fragrant and sweet, and the mochi are delicious. The taste is smoky and tough, and the texture is contrasting. It is a specialty snack of Cheung Chau that cannot be missed.





Late-night snacks


Cheung Chau Night Fair

Night Fair

After 10 p.m., the area along the Cheung Chau Pier will transform into a Cheung Chau Night Fair. The late-night snack stall offers a variety of Hong Kong-style snacks, such as fried chicken thighs, handmade instant rice rolls, curry fish balls, siu mai, etc. There are many types of food to choose from, and they are Cheung Chau snacks that you must not miss.

- Beancurd Factory -


As old shops disappear, Hong Kong’s bean factories become increasingly valuable.
Speaking of beancurd factory in Hong Kong, most people will mention Kung Wo Beancurd Factory recommended by Michelin. But there is another beancurd factory that originated in Sham Shui Po and has an equally long history that few people mention. I am referring to Shun Hing Lung Kwai Kee Beancurd Factory.







Shun Hing Lung Kwai Kee Beancurd Factory has a strong nostalgic atmosphere and is a rare old shop in Hong Kong.

Shun Hing Lung bean-making method is inherited from the 80-year-old brand. In 1980, the founder established his own brand Shun Hing Lung from his family's old store.






In addition to retaining the nostalgic flavor, Shun Hing Lung Kwai Kee Beancurd Factory also retains the same decorative design and human touch.
